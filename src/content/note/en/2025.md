## Life

In 2025, thinking about it, the biggest change in my life is that I started smoking.

I used to have the same attitude towards smoking as most people: not exactly hateful, but definitely not fond of it. I started drinking very early and felt that the slight intoxication after drinking had many benefits for emotional release; but for smoking, I firmly believed that it was harmful and useless, a behavior I would absolutely never touch.

But now, my attitude has changed. Smoking is still harmful, but I no longer feel it is "useless." Sacrificing a little health to bring a moment of dopamine to my brain and exchange for a few minutes of peace seems like a good deal to me now.

Perhaps having experienced too much after entering university, the wash of reality has changed my attitude towards many things. The road ahead is not smooth sailing, and the so-called university life is not as flat and smooth as I previously thought.

I might be becoming a little pessimistic bit by bit.

Being a sophomore, an age that should be full of high spirits, I often fall into confusion, not knowing where my way out is, and unsure if I can live the life I yearn for in the future. The more I look into it, the more I feel that the road ahead is like a thick fog that cannot be dissolved, causing endless worry.

And what sustains me through these anxious moments is perhaps this brief paralysis brought by nicotine. However, of course, I will not instigate people around me to smoke. It has always been a bad thing in my opinion, but since I am already trapped in it, I can only calmly accept this price.

My addiction to smoking is actually not strong. During a certain period in the middle, I also tried to quit smoking, but what quitting brought was endless emptiness. Whether it was on the way from the dormitory to the classroom, or alone on the balcony late at night, as long as I was alone, I would help myself to light one. Watching the smoke rise seems to temporarily fill the hole in my heart.

Writing this, I suddenly realized that what really made me smoke is actually just loneliness.

I consider myself used to being alone, accustomed to the feeling of coming and going alone, but when I really came to a strange city, away from my family and familiar circles, I realized that I had not truly adapted to this bone-deep loneliness.

I actually made many friends in university, but they are all so-called acquaintances. Except for being relatively familiar with the three roommates in the dormitory (~~my dear sons~~) and being able to joke around, most of the rest are just at the level of meeting and greeting, maintaining superficial politeness. Thinking carefully again, it seems that since junior high school, I have never met that kind of close friend who can talk about anything and truly resonate with my soul.

However, in this life, it is inherently difficult to meet a confidant. Meeting is fate, and missing out is also normal. Thinking about it carefully, I feel relieved haha.

Life hasn't changed much this year. I'm still busy every day as always, yet I don't know what I'm busy with. A day passes in the blink of an eye. I've thought about changing countless times and made plans, but my self-control and execution are almost zero. Watching my family and siblings work, get married, and step into the next stage of life, I can only smile bitterly and sigh: time flies so fast.

## Technology

In 2025, has there been technical progress? Yes, but not much.

This year I learned about many previously unfamiliar fields, such as client-side development, frontend, etc. My horizons have indeed broadened, but my hard skills haven't grown much.

Influenced by the AI flood, many times I no longer write code independently, but direct AI to work. Although this allows me to quickly produce products and demos in unfamiliar fields, I rarely delve into technical details, and I am even vague about the implementation logic of the entire function.

This year I took over a React Native project. Because it was my first contact, I had to bite the bullet. Although it was successfully launched in the end, there were many bugs. Reviewing it carefully now, I took many detours. In the new year, I plan to use Flutter to refactor it, trying not to rely on AI, hoping to bring a better experience to users. (But the urgent task is to fix the old bugs first!)

In the second semester, I participated in ByteDance's engineering training camp. Originally, I just saw the registration and wrote a resume to try my luck, but I didn't expect to be selected. The group assigned to me, both the mentor and the members, were very good, and everyone was working hard. In the end, I unexpectedly got a direct pass to the final interview (although I failed in the end QAQ).

Speaking of this interview, this was the first formal interview in my life. The interviewer was very nice; he was the mentor responsible for our group in the training camp. During the interview, he asked many technical details and underlying implementation questions around my resume, ~~and this precisely hit my pain points~~. Even if I couldn't answer, he didn't pressure me. Although I failed, I think this is a very precious experience, which made me truly discover my own shortcomings and loopholes.

## Outlook 2026?

2026, I think the primary task is to settle down.

Whether it is technology or studies, improving my personal ability is the most important thing. ~~Also, there is robotics which I have neglected for a long time.~~

In the new year, I won't set any grand goals. Finally, I wish everyone a Happy New Year!
